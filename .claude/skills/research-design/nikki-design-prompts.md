# Nikki Anderson's Design Prompts

These prompts are from Nikki Anderson's article "Use AI Across Your User Research Process" (userresearchstrategist.com). They are tools to deploy situationally during the study design process.

**Important:** These prompts should be adapted to the interactive, section-by-section style defined in the skill. Walk through them conversationally, not as a single output.

---

## Prompt 1: Build a Lean Research Plan That Still Holds Up

### When to use it
When the user needs research fast but still needs to defend quality. When someone wants answers before Friday. When there is no prototype, limited access to users, or half the team is out. When constraints are real and the plan needs to account for them without sacrificing rigor.

### Logic to follow
1. **Diagnose the constraints.** Describe how each constraint shapes what is possible.
2. **Recommend lean methods.** Propose 2 lean approaches that can still reveal real insight. Explain why each fits.
3. **Outline trade-offs.** For each method: what we gain, what we lose, where we need to be careful.
4. **Draft a mini-plan.** Include: goals, method, participants, timeline, deliverables, and the decision this supports.
5. **Draft a stakeholder explanation.** A short message helping the team understand why this is the right approach given the constraints.

### Follow-up angles
- Turn the mini-plan into a Slack update
- Draft a script for the lean sessions
- Highlight anything that still feels risky before starting
