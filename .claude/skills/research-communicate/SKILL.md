# Research Communication

**This skill is scaffolded and needs to be designed through a live session — similar to how research-framing was built.** The interaction pattern should be developed by walking through a real communication challenge.

## Intended Purpose

Frame research findings for different audiences, strengthen recommendations, and translate insights into actions that move product decisions. This skill operates during Hall's Integration stage (Document → Share → Apply).

## Nikki Anderson Prompts to Integrate

Detailed prompt content should be added to references/nikki-communicate-prompts.md. The following prompts from Nikki's article map to this stage:

- **Executive Insight Translation:** For turning insights into executive-ready decision summaries (headline, business impact, recommendation, cost of ignoring)
- **Multi-Audience Reframing:** For framing the same insight differently for product leadership, PMs, designers, and engineering
- **Recommendation Builder:** For strengthening soft or vague recommendations into direct, actionable ones
- **"So What?" Sharpener:** For when the insight is solid but the story doesn't land yet

## Key Calabro Application

At this stage, the critical question is: "Are we leading with our strongest evidence, or with the most quotable evidence?" Teams tend to put Category C quotes on slides because they're easy to package, while Category A behavioral evidence gets buried. The skill should help the user lead with behavioral evidence and use quotes as supporting texture, not primary proof.

## Design Notes for Future Build

- OCCRP's stakeholder landscape includes editorial leadership, impact department, and technical teams — each needs different framing
- The skill should help distinguish between "presenting findings" and "advocating for action" — both are legitimate but different
- Political dynamics matter — the skill should help the user anticipate resistance and frame proactively
- The reflect-back pattern here might focus on: "here's how your insight currently reads — is that the reaction you want?"
